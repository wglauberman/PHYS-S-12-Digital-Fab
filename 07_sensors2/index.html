### Week 7: More Sensors For this week, we were tasked with making an output sensor and analyzing it and eventually using it with an input sensor. The output sensor I chose is the buzzer because it makes cool sounds. Here is a demonstration of it: ![video of embroidery](buzzertest.mov) I think it is a pretty cool thing. I also got some photos of attaching this buzzer to an osciloscope: ![image of embroidery](osci.jpg) ![image of embroidery](osci2.jpg) Based on voltages and time measurements, you can change how wide the x axis x (horizontal distortion) and the y axis (vertical distortion). There is about 2.5 beeps every 1 millisecond on the graphs. The note played in the first one is C4 (262Hz) and the second was D#4/Eb4 (312Hz). To combine an input sensor and an output sensor, I decided to use the copper stickers again since they were easy to work with. I got it so when you pressed them together the song wouldn't play, but when they were separated the song would play again: ![image of embroidery](copperbuzzersetup.jpg) ![video of embroidery](copperbuzzer.mov) This was just done with combining the "note code" from the doom song and my copper code from last time.
