### Week 2: Motor Design After laser cutting and re-learning Fusion 360, the next task was to involve a motor in something that moves. My idea was to use a motor and a gear to make it appear like an object was moving along a circle. First, I found this wood box roughly measuing 30mm by 10mm: ![image of embroidery](bricknew.jpeg) Then, I quickly modeled the rings of the course and downloaded a gear on a Fusion 360 add in. Here is one of the rings: ![image of embroidery](outermodel.png) After retrieving a motor and laser-cutting the pieces, this is what it looked like before being put together: ![image of embroidery](pieces.jpeg) To put it all together, I drilled a hole to put a wooden rod in the brick piece. In the smaller, noncentered hole of the gear in the previous image, I stuck it there and hot glued it into place. I then hot glued the bigger and smaller rings to the face of the gear to resemble a "track". Finally, I hot glued the motor into the center hole, and in the end, it ended up looking like this: ![image of embroidery](finalproductnew.jpg) ![image of embroidery](finalproductbacknew.jpg) The current motor I was using was too small, so I replace it with a motor that fit perfectly in the gear hole spot. To make the "machine" stand on its own without my help. I laser cut a box with holes on the top and side so that the motor and wires could fit in: ![image of embroidery](woodenbox.jpg) After all that, here is a video showcasing the movement of my block on a track: ![video of embroidery](motorspin.mov) ### Week 2: Arduino and Motors I wanted to implement my previous project into this new project, so I connected an arduino to a motor driver to my motor and made it spin at various speeds. My drawing of the circuit looks like this ![image of embroidery](circuitsketch.jpeg) Here is the setup in the real world: ![image of embroidery](arduinosetup.jpg) and here it is in action: ![video of embroidery](motorandarduino.mov) I have also pasted the Arduino code here. Basically what it is doing is starting off slow in one direction, gradually picking up pace, and stopping after ~12 seconds. Then it would do the same thing in reverse. The reason I had it pick up speed is because my block is supposed to be "running around the track" and so I'm trying to make it seem like it starts out lightly jogging, and moves into full on running. void setup() { pinMode(A1A, OUTPUT); pinMode(A1B, OUTPUT); analogWrite(A1A, 0); digitalWrite(A1B, LOW); } void loop() { analogWrite(A1A, 100); digitalWrite(A1B, LOW); delay(4000); analogWrite(A1A, 150); delay(4000); analogWrite(A1A, 200); delay(4000); analogWrite(A1A, 0); delay(2500); digitalWrite(A1B, HIGH); analogWrite(A1A, 255-100); delay(4000); analogWrite(A1A, 255-150); delay(4000); analogWrite(A1A, 255-200); delay(4000); analogWrite(A1A, 0); digitalWrite(A1B, LOW); delay(2500); }
